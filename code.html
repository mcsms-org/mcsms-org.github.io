Code of Conduct

Adopted: May 27, 2026 | Version 1.0

1. Definitions

  • Committee: The elected officers of the Society.
  • TIC: The Teacher-in-Charge appointed by the College.

2. Scope and Applicability

This Code applies to all Society activities, including physical meetings, workshops, GitHub interactions, email, and other official communication channels (e.g., WhatsApp). The Society welcomes members of all skill levels, backgrounds, and identities. Discrimination or exclusion based on these characteristics is a violation of this Code.

3. Adherence to School Rules

All members must follow the Maliyadeva College Code of Conduct at all times. Any violation of school rules will be handled by the College administration.

4. Academic Honesty

Open collaboration and mutual learning are encouraged. However, academic integrity requires that credit be given where it is due.

  • Members are encouraged to:
    • Discuss ideas, help others debug, and share resources.
    • Use existing open-source code, libraries, or tutorials as part of a project.
  • Members must:
    • Clearly attribute any external code, ideas, or assistance in project documentation.
    • Never present someone else’s work as their own.
  • AI Tools: AI usage must be in accordance with the AI Policy defined in the Bylaws. AI-assisted logic must be attributable and explainable; inability to explain will be treated as academic dishonesty.

5. Digital Conduct

  • Use the Society’s GitHub organization and online spaces responsibly. Do not delete or modify others’ work without permission.
  • Do not share credentials with non-members.
  • Report security issues to the Committee immediately.

6. Harassment and Retaliation

The Society is committed to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone. Harassment includes offensive comments, intimidation, deliberate exclusion, and unwelcome physical contact. Retaliation against anyone who reports a violation in good faith is prohibited and will be treated as a serious violation.

7. Reporting and Confidentiality

If you experience or witness a violation, contact any Committee member or the TIC.

  • Confidentiality: Reports will be shared only with those necessary to resolve the matter.
  • Anonymous Reporting: Reports may be submitted in writing anonymously to any Committee member or the TIC. These will be investigated as thoroughly as possible.
  • False Reporting: Knowingly making a false report against another member is a violation of this Code and may result in disciplinary action against the reporter.
  • Emergency: If someone is in immediate danger, contact a teacher or school authority first, then notify the Committee.

8. Enforcement and Appeals

  • Minor infractions: Verbal warning from an officer.
  • Repeated/Serious violations: The Committee will review the case and may suspend or terminate membership, following the process in the Society Bylaws.
  • Appeal: Affected members may appeal to the TIC within one week. The TIC’s decision is final.
