What Is Modeling and Simulation?

Learn the basics of modeling and simulation, including the three types of models—physical, virtual, and mathematical—and the three types of simulations: live, virtual, and constructive. Discover how Simulink® is used to bring mathematical models and virtual simulations together, helping engineers apply these concepts to design and validate complex systems.

Vocabulary

Simpler Terms for Complex Ideas

We use some big words, but the ideas are simple. Here is what they actually mean:

  • Fluid & Air Flow: Simulating how liquids and gases move (like air over a wing).
  • Disease Tracking: Using math to predict how infections spread through a group.
  • Particle Systems: Simulating how many individual objects (like stars or atoms) interact.
  • N-Body Simulation: Calculating the motion of many objects pulling on each other.

New to Coding? Don't worry. We have a Beginner's First Simulation project specifically designed for people who have never written a line of code.
